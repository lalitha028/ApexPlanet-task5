Boeing 747 - The Queen of the Skies

Overview

The Boeing 747, known as the "Queen of the Skies", is one of the most iconic aircraft in aviation history. First introduced in 1970, it revolutionized air travel by making long-haul flights accessible to the masses. The 747 is recognized worldwide for its distinctive hump on the upper deck.

Specifications

🛩 Manufacturer: Boeing
🛩 Type: Passenger wide-body jet
🛩 Max speed: 614 mph (988 km/h)
🛩 Range: 8,000 miles (12,875 km)
🛩 Seating capacity: ~416 (typical three-class), up to 660 (all economy)
🛩 Wingspan: 224 ft 7 in (68.4 m)
🛩 First flight: February 9, 1969

Boarding Process

✅ Check-in (online or at counter)
✅ Security screening
✅ Proceed to boarding gate (typically via jet bridge)
✅ Upper deck passengers may board through a dedicated jet bridge
✅ Locate assigned seat and stow luggage
✅ Fasten seatbelt and prepare for take-off

Fun Facts

✈ Over 0 Boeing 747s have been built.
✈ The 747 was the first airplane dubbed a "Jumbo Jet".
✈ Its upper deck was initially designed as a lounge or additional seating.
✈ Some 747s are converted into cargo freighters and even airborne command centers!
